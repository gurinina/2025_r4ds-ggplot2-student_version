Think-Pair-Share: How We'll Work on Exercises

Throughout this course, we'll use a collaborative learning approach called Think-Pair-Share for many of our coding exercises. This method helps you learn more effectively by giving you time to think, discuss with peers, and share ideas with the class.

The Three Steps

1THINK (2-3 minutes)

Work individually and silently

  • Read the exercise carefully
  • Look at the starter code (if provided)
  • Think about how you would approach the problem
  • Write down your ideas or start coding
  • It's okay if you're not sure! This is thinking time, not "get it perfect" time

2PAIR (3-5 minutes)

Discuss with your neighbor

  • Turn to your partner and share your ideas
  • Explain your approach: "I was thinking we could..."
  • Listen to their approach: "What did you try?"
  • Work together to agree on a solution
  • Try out your code together
  • Both people should understand the solution before moving on

3SHARE (5-10 minutes)

Present to the class as a pair

  • I'll call on pairs (not individuals!) to share
  • One or both of you can explain your solution
  • We'll type it together and discuss as a class
  • Other pairs will build on or refine the ideas
  • Every pair's contribution helps everyone learn

Example: Adding Labels to a Plot

Exercise: Add informative labels to this plot

THINK (2 min): What pattern do you see? What would make a good title?

PAIR (3 min): Discuss with your neighbor what labels to add

SHARE (5 min): Each pair will share their title and we'll discuss which ones work best

During THINK time, you might think:

  • "I see that compact cars have higher MPG than pickups..."
  • "My title could be: 'Compact cars are more fuel efficient'"
  • "I need to add axis labels and a caption too..."

During PAIR time, you might discuss:

  • "What title did you come up with?"
  • "Should we be more specific about the numbers?"
  • "How about 'Compact cars have >10 higher highway MPG than pickups'?"

During SHARE time:

  • Your pair presents your title
  • We discuss: Is it specific? Does it describe a finding or just the plot type?
  • We combine the best ideas from all pairs

Tips for Success

During THINK time:

  • Don't be afraid to experiment - this is your time to explore
  • Write down your ideas even if you're not 100% sure
  • Look at the starter code and think about what needs to be added

During PAIR time:

  • Both people should contribute - take turns explaining
  • Ask questions: "Why did you do it that way?"
  • If you disagree, try both approaches and see what works
  • Make sure you both understand before Share time

During SHARE time:

  • Be ready - I'll call on every pair at some point!
  • It's okay if your solution isn't perfect - that's how we learn
  • Listen to other pairs' ideas - you might learn a better approach
  • Ask questions if you don't understand someone's approach

Remember

The goal isn't to get it perfect on your own. The goal is to learn together! Think-Pair-Share ensures everyone has time to think, everyone gets to discuss with a peer, and everyone contributes to our collective understanding.
